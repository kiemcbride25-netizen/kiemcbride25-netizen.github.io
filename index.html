Executive Summary
The AI infrastructure buildout has created an unexpected chokepoint: semiconductor packaging, not chip design or fabrication, is now the binding constraint on how many AI accelerators can be shipped. TSMC's CEO has stated CoWoS packaging capacity is "sold out through 2026." NVIDIA has pre-booked 50%+ of that capacity. The packaging market will grow from $52B in 2025 to $90B+ by 2031.
Within this theme, Amkor Technology (AMKR) is the single most differentiated investment. It is the only US-based OSAT at scale, is building a $7B Arizona facility with Apple as its anchor customer and TSMC as its next-door neighbor, and management is targeting revenues of $11B with $5+ EPS by 2030 — a 3.3× earnings increase from 2025 actual EPS of $1.50. Critically, the company recently acquired 67 additional acres for a Phase 2 Arizona expansion that is not included in any current management guidance or sell-side model.
Three-Bullet Investment Case
1. The bottleneck is packaging. Everyone owns TSMC and NVIDIA. The infrastructure that makes those chips physically shippable — advanced packaging — is undersupplied and underfollowed. AMKR is the picks-and-shovels play that most investors have missed.
2. Geopolitical moat no one else has. AMKR is the only scaled OSAT building in the US. Apple and TSMC Arizona need a US-based packaging partner to complete a domestic end-to-end supply chain. AMKR is that partner — and likely will be for a decade.
3. Earnings are set to 3× — and models don't capture the full story. Management targets $5 EPS by 2030. Phase 2 Arizona (171 acres acquired, not yet in guidance) could add another $3–5B in revenue capacity. At $5 EPS × 24x = $120. At $6 EPS (Phase 2 ramp) × 22x = $132.

Why packaging is harder than it sounds
AI GPUs require CoWoS (Chip-on-Wafer-on-Substrate) packaging — an extremely complex process that co-integrates logic dies and High Bandwidth Memory (HBM) on a single silicon interposer. It's the reason NVIDIA's H100 and B100 chips can have 80GB+ of memory bandwidth.
›18-month lead times on ABF substrates (the carrier under every AI chip). AI chips consume 10× more ABF than a PC chip.
›NVIDIA books 50%+ of TSMC's CoWoS capacity through 2026 — and even that isn't enough to meet demand.
›4× capacity expansion underway at TSMC (35K → 130K wafers/month by end of 2026). Still won't fully clear the backlog.
›Packaging ≠ fabrication. The skills, equipment, and facilities are entirely different. You can't just redirect a fab line.
Market sizing: Advanced packaging grows from $52B (2025) → $90B+ by 2031 at 9.4% CAGR. AI-specific packaging alone: $3.4B → $9.8B by 2034. OSAT top 10 control 75% of the market.

AMKR Deep Dive — Why This Is the Best Expression of the Trade
Business Overview
Amkor Technology is the world's second-largest OSAT (Outsourced Semiconductor Assembly and Test) company. Founded in 1968, it provides packaging, assembly, and testing services to nearly every major semiconductor company — Apple, NVIDIA, AMD, Qualcomm, Broadcom, and more.
›Revenue mix: ~40% advanced packaging (growing), ~35% wirebond, ~25% test services
›Customers: Apple (~20% of revenue), NVIDIA, AMD, Qualcomm, Broadcom
›Key geography: Korea, Vietnam, Malaysia, and now — critically — Arizona, USA
The Arizona Thesis — What Makes AMKR Unique
›$7B Phase 1 investment in Peoria, Arizona — first advanced packaging facility of this scale in the US
›Apple is the anchor customer — packaging Apple-designed chips (A-series, M-series) fabricated at TSMC's Phoenix fabs
›TSMC partnership — co-located next to TSMC Phoenix. Enables end-to-end US chip production for the first time
›CHIPS Act alignment — only US OSAT receiving strategic support. Near-impossible to replicate within 5+ years
›Phase 2: 67 additional acres acquired (May 2026) — not in guidance, not in any sell-side model

Key Risks & Mitigants
Risk 1: Arizona Execution Delays Building a greenfield advanced packaging fab is complex. Delays in ramp timeline could push EPS below model. Mitigant: AMKR has built 20+ fabs globally. Apple's economic interest in a smooth ramp provides operational alignment.
Risk 2: AI Capex Slowdown If hyperscaler AI spending decelerates, packaging demand drops with it. Mitigant: $725B 2026 AI capex has multi-year contracts underpinning it. AMKR's backlog provides 12+ months of visibility.
Risk 3: TSMC Internalizes More Packaging TSMC is expanding its own CoWoS capacity, which could reduce OSAT share. Mitigant: TSMC and AMKR Arizona have a formal partnership — TSMC needs AMKR for US-based finishing. These are complementary, not competitive.
Risk 4: Customer Concentration (Apple ~20%) Apple softness could pressure revenue. Mitigant: AI data center is diversifying the mix rapidly. Apple share of revenue is expected to decline as a percentage as hyperscaler and AI work scales.
